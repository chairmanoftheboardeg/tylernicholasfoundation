A Message From The Chairman

"Philanthropy must evolve beyond awareness. It must become infrastructure."

The Tyler Nicholas Foundation was not built to simply talk about the problems facing Seychelles; it was built to aggressively solve them. During my time in public service, I realized that while government policy is essential for the future, our communities and our youth need tangible help today.

This Foundation is a multi-disciplinary action group. We do not restrict ourselves to one single issue because the challenges facing our islands are deeply interconnected. You cannot solve economic stagnation without addressing digital literacy; you cannot protect our coastal environments without empowering the youth who will inherit them.

From securing international work attachments for ambitious students, to funding grassroots climate action, to providing free digital lifelines for local artisans—our mandate is vast, but our methodology is precise. We are here to act as a shadow-infrastructure for the Republic, providing the tools, the network, and the capital to ensure no Seychellois is left behind.

Our Scope of Work

A Comprehensive National Mandate

We operate across multiple sectors to provide a holistic safety net for the Republic. Explore our primary pillars of action below.

Career & Opportunity Placement

We actively source and secure customized work attachments, long-term internships, and international holiday opportunities for Seychellois youth by contacting organizations and leveraging our corporate network directly on their behalf.

Apply for Placement →

Environmental Stewardship & Climate Action

Executing grassroots coastal protection, organizing massive youth-led mangrove restoration projects, and providing micro-grants for local eco-innovation and sustainability startups.

Digital Literacy & Enterprise Incubator

Through "The Artisan Initiative" and our corporate partners, we provide free websites, digital branding, and coding workshops to local vendors and underprivileged youth to transition Seychelles into a creator economy.

Health, Mental Wellness & Food Security

Maintaining the National Mental Health Directory, organizing preventative wellness campaigns, and partnering closely with FoodShare Seychelles to manage community food logistics for vulnerable demographics.

Civic Education & Policy Research Lab

Operating an Open-Access Education Library for students, conducting youth parliament simulations, and aggregating community data to support evidence-based, data-driven national policymaking.

Cultural Heritage & Social Inclusion

Running "The Generations Archive" to digitally preserve the folklore of our elderly, while providing strict advocacy and support networks for persons with disabilities and marginalized groups.
